  • SYZYGY

    Solar and lunar eclipses take place when the Sun, Earth, and Moon are aligned. This type of event is also called a syzygy. During a solar eclipse, the Moon blocks the view of the Sun as seen from Earth. Solar eclipses can be central or partial. Lunar eclipses happen when Earth positions itself between the sun and the moon, casting a shadow across the lunar surface. it can only occur during a full moon and are a popular event for skywatchers worldwide as they do not require any specialist equipment to enjoy (unlike solar eclipses).
